Daydream Inc.

A pharmaceutical company that churns out fantastical potions that grant wishes, through methods no one can fathom.

The raw material is ghost stories.

……This was the extent of the record when this corporation first appeared in the <Darkness Exploration Record>.

The lack of explanation invited further imagination; its bizarre technological prowess, mystery, cruelty, and the blanks in its history felt chilling.

However, as the Wiki grows, the breadcrumbs accumulate accordingly.

‘Hidden lore gets added.’

Naturally, Daydream Inc. couldn't have just fallen out of the sky with perfectly formed, unknown pharmaceutical technology.

Even if it appeared that way, if one dug deep enough, its origins and history had to be deducible…. That was what made it thrilling and fun.

Eventually, as time passed, significant exploration records were added here and there within the Darkness Exploration Record, allowing readers to form several conspiracy theories and make deductions about Daydream.

—What exactly is Daydream Inc.?

And the place I had just visited… was evidence for Conspiracy Theory Number 2.

Conspiracy Theory 2: Daydream Inc. was not originally a pharmaceutical company.

Specifically, the original source of Daydream Inc.’s pharmaceutical technology.

“…Whew.”

Swallowing hard, I began to look around the ‘Dream Incubation Room.’

The dust-covered laboratory looked like a science room in a closed-down school, or an old, abandoned lab after the apocalypse.

Everything looked dead.

Workbenches and experimental equipment, beakers, and display cases containing unknown liquids.

And… a massive machine in the center.

“…….”

It was an old, 2000s-style experimental machine with buttons and a keyboard panel beneath an empty glass tube that didn't light up.

Dream incubator

And the experiment log lying beside it.

It was the very thing mentioned in the Exploration Records.

I unfolded the old paper and read—

Every single page.

The experimental records, which must have been written in neat characters originally, were thoroughly crushed and smeared one by one. Filth, blood, ink, and pencil marks—screams and madness were stuck thick and sticky onto the pages….

“Ugh.”

I set down the experiment log as if throwing it away.

Then I caught my breath.

‘I thought I could handle it.’

I was wrong.

The reason I gave up without restoring the text buried beneath wasn't some <Darkness Exploration Record> technique of building atmosphere by blocking information; it was because it was truly unbearable to look at.
